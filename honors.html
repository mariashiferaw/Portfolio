Honors Program

Why honors?

Everyone has their own positive traits and life achievements, but not everyone is aware of how to showcase their best attributes. Prior to my sophomore year at MNSU, I was unaware of the honors program. My friend came over at that point and told me about the advantages of taking the class. I then made the decision to apply for the program. I was turned down. I reapplied for the next semester because I was so excited to attend the course, and this time I got accepted.
My honors advisor's advice helped me better comprehend the purpose of the entire program and how I may advance my career. Even though I have been involved in a variety of activities, events, leadership roles, and research projects, I was unsure of how to continue building on my current successes. We are going to develop our portfolios, which helps me develop while also learning more about myself. By allowing me time to work on the things I need to, it can help me identify my strengths and flaws. I want to be able to use these attributes, whether in class or at events, to increase the impact of my contribution once I have a solid grasp of how I may develop my mission, values, and competences. I'll attempt to make the most of my college experience using the information I've learned. I wish to graduate with honors and go on to actively participate in activities that benefit society and present oneself with confidence. In addition, I want to be open to improvement and aware of my weaknesses. Being fiercely competitive when giving treatment and assistance for my patients will help me in my future profession. Having those excellent characteristics, I can assist my patients in doing their best. I have been practicing professional communication and teamwork throughout my undergraduate experience; therefore, I am able to work with my team in a professional manner.
Overall, I think this is a great chance for me to grow personally based on my prior experience and comprehend what it means for me. It will enable me to interact more effectively and positively with my classmates, neighborhood, family, and other people. I'll do my best to take advantage of every chance I get in college to advance and direct myself. I'm excited about all the opportunities, experiences, and events that will come my way so that I can make a significant life change.
